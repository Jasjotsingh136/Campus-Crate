CampusCrate arises from a persistent and recurring problem within college
and university hostels. Students frequently undergo transitions such as admissions,
room changes, semester breaks, and graduation, during which they need
to buy or sell essential items like furniture, electronics, books, and daily-use goods.
However, existing methods such as WhatsApp groups, Telegram channels,
and external platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace are unorganized, unreliable, and often unsafe. 
These channels lack verification mechanisms, structured listings, and dispute resolution systems,
leading to problems such as scams, poor coordination, time wastage, and inconvenience. 
Additionally, many usable items are abandoned or discarded, contributing to waste and clutter within hostels.
Hostel administrations also face challenges in managing leftover items and space utilization. 
This situation clearly indicates a gap in the market for a structured,
trustworthy, and campus-specific exchange system that can streamline transactions and improve efficiency
